Project Overview

Accessible Places Assistant is a platform designed to help people with disabilities identify and access public places that are suitable for their specific needs. The system evaluates places based on accessibility features such as ramps, accessible toilets, resting areas, parking facilities, and other mobility-friendly services.

The Problem We Address

Many public places such as hospitals, railway stations, government offices, and other important facilities lack proper accessibility information. People with mobility challenges often face difficulties in knowing whether a place is suitable before visiting it. This creates dependence on others and reduces their independence.

Why This Project Was Implemented

This project was developed to bridge the gap between accessibility requirements and available information. By providing accessibility scores and detailed facility information, users can make informed decisions and confidently plan their visits.

The platform also connects users with volunteers who can provide assistance whenever additional support is required.

How It Works

1. User Registration

Users create a profile and specify their accessibility requirements.

2. Accessibility Evaluation

Places are analysed based on important accessibility parameters.

3. Accessibility Score

The system provides a score indicating how suitable a location is for the user.

4. Volunteer Assistance

Users can request support from registered volunteers when required.

Key Features

  • Accessibility scoring based on user requirements
  • Information about facilities available at public places
  • Volunteer assistance system for additional support
  • User-friendly platform for people with disabilities
  • Promotes independence and inclusive mobility

Our Vision

Our vision is to create a society where accessibility information is easily available and individuals with disabilities can travel, access services, and participate in daily activities with greater confidence and independence.
